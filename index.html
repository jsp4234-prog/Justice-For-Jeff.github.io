Case Summary

Jeff Patterson is a former United States Postal Service (USPS) City Carrier Technician who is proceeding pro se in a federal civil rights action. According to the operative complaint, the lawsuit asserts claims under the Rehabilitation Act, the Age Discrimination in Employment Act (ADEA), and related anti-retaliation provisions of federal law.

Mr. Patterson contends that after sustaining a work-related spinal injury, he received documented medical restrictions limiting his work hours and certain physical activities. The complaint alleges that USPS management entered into a written modified-duty agreement acknowledging these restrictions.

Injury and Accommodation (Allegations)

Court filings state that in November 2021, Mr. Patterson sustained a lumbar spine injury while performing his duties as a career carrier. Medical providers diagnosed a chronic spinal condition and imposed specific limitations, including limits on daily work hours and physical exertion.

According to the complaint, in February 2022 Mr. Patterson and USPS management executed a written modified-duty assignment reflecting these medical restrictions. Plaintiff alleges that this agreement was intended to remain in effect unless formally revised in writing.

Alleged Workplace Conduct

The complaint further alleges that beginning in January 2023, USPS management repeatedly assigned Mr. Patterson work in excess of the documented medical limitations. Plaintiff contends that these assignments occurred despite repeated notifications of the restrictions and without issuance of a revised written accommodation.

According to the pleadings, time-and-attendance records are cited to support the allegation that work hours exceeded prescribed limits on multiple occasions.

Discipline and Termination (Disputed)

Mr. Patterson alleges that the excessive work assignments aggravated his medical condition and resulted in medically necessary absences. The complaint asserts that these absences were subsequently cited as the basis for progressive discipline, including suspensions and, ultimately, termination of employment in 2024.

USPS disputes these allegations and denies liability.

Personal Context

Court filings and plaintiff’s narrative state that these events occurred during a period when Mr. Patterson was providing care for his spouse. The complaint includes allegations related to protected leave and retaliation. These allegations are disputed.

Additional Allegations

The lawsuit also alleges that discriminatory or retaliatory statements were made relating to age, disability, and anticipated use of protected medical leave. These allegations are contested by the defendant.

Procedural Status

The case is pending before the United States District Court for the Central District of California. Motion practice and discovery are ongoing. No findings of fact or liability have been made by the Court.
